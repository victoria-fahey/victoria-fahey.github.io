Neuroplasticity & Growth Mindset



Neuroplasticity


What is neuroplasticity & how does it benefit people?

Neuroplasticity is defined as the brains ability to modify, change, and adapt both structure and function throughout life and in response to experiences. It is the understanding that our brains are malleable and through repeated practice and experiences, anyone can learn anything.

Understanding this concept that our brains are not just static provides many benefits, it allows your brain to adapt and change which can help influence:

  • The ability to learn new things
  • The ability to enhance your existing cognitive capabilities
  • Recovery from strokes and traumatic brain injuries
  • Strengthening areas if some functions are lost or in decline
  • Improvements that can promote brain fitness

What are some ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

Luckily for us, there are things we can do to help encourage our brain to adapt and change and to increase our neuroplasticity. These include:

  • Enriching your environment – introducing focused attention, novelty and challenge has been shown to stimulate positive changes in the brain. For example:
    • Learning something new such as a new language, instrument, or even coding 😉
    • Travelling & exploring new places
    • Getting creative
    • Reading
  • Getting plenty of rest – sleep plays an important role of growth in the brain and strengthens neural connections
  • Exercise

Practice makes Perfect
 the more times the network is stimulated, the stronger and more efficient it becomes

Growth Mindset


What is growth mindset & why is it relevant?

Mindset is used to describe the way people think about their own ability and talent. These can be categorised as either a fixed or growth mindset. A fixed mindset is the belief that your basic abilities, intelligence, and talent are fixed traits that are unchangeable. Whereas, a growth mindset is the power of believing you can improve and your ability can be developed.

A growth mindset is extremely relevant to learning as it allows you to overcome obstacles and failure you may face when learning something new or developing a new skill. Growth mindsets understand the importance of persistence and determination. For example, when faced with a challenging problem to solve, someone with a fixed mindset may give up easily as they don’t believe they have the smarts to overcome this problem. Whereas a growth mindset may see this as an opportunity for improvement where they can solve the problem, just not yet.

By changing the way you think, you can change the way you learn
